# Ashes of Aeloria: Game Design Document

## Executive Summary

**Ashes of Aeloria** is a story-driven civilization rebuilding game that combines tactical turn-based strategy with deep narrative progression. Players emerge from an ancient stasis chamber into a world devastated by "The Sundering" and must guide the remnants of civilization through recovery, growth, and ultimately transcendence.

The game blends strategic resource management, diplomatic intrigue, moral decision-making, and epic storytelling into a cohesive experience that spans from desperate survival to god-like power.

---

## Core Game Concept

### Theme
**"From Ashes to Transcendence"** - The journey from the ruins of a fallen empire to building something greater than what came before.

### Genre
- Turn-based strategy with 4X elements (eXplore, eXpand, eXploit, eXterminate)
- Narrative-driven civilization builder
- Choice-driven RPG elements

### Target Audience
- Strategy game enthusiasts who enjoy Civilization, Total War, or Crusader Kings
- Players who appreciate deep narrative and meaningful choices
- Fans of post-apocalyptic rebuilding themes
- Adults (16+) who can appreciate complex moral decisions

## Game World & Setting

### The World of Aeloria

**Before the Sundering:**
- A magnificent empire spanning multiple continents
- Advanced magical and technological civilization
- Ruled by the Architects - beings of immense power and wisdom
- Golden age of art, science, and enlightenment

**The Sundering:**
- A catastrophic event that shattered reality itself
- Caused by hubris, magical experimentation, or external forces (varies by player interpretation)
- Left the world scarred, twisted, and barely habitable
- Most Architects died; survivors entered stasis to wait for recovery

**Present Day:**
- 1,000 years after the Sundering
- You emerge as the last Architect from stasis
- Scattered survivor communities struggle in the wasteland
- Corruption spreads like a plague across the land
- Nature is wounded but slowly healing
- Ancient knowledge lies buried in ruins

### Geography & Environment

**Biome Types:**
- **Sundered Wastelands** - Twisted, magical radiation zones
- **Dead Forests** - Skeletal remains of once-great woodlands
- **Corruption Zones** - Areas where reality itself is warped
- **Survivor Settlements** - Pockets of struggling civilization
- **Ancient Ruins** - Remnants of the old empire
- **Healing Lands** - Areas where nature is recovering
- **Sanctuaries** - Rare pristine zones protected by old magic

## Core Gameplay Loop

### Turn Structure
1. **Planning Phase** - Review events, make strategic decisions
2. **Action Phase** - Deploy commanders, build, research, diplomacy
3. **Resolution Phase** - Combat, construction, random events
4. **Story Phase** - Narrative events, consequences, progression

### Primary Activities

**Exploration & Expansion:**
- Send commanders to explore unknown territories
- Discover ancient ruins, survivor settlements, resources
- Map the extent of the damage and opportunities
- Establish new settlements and outposts

**Resource Management:**
- **Knowledge** - Recovered wisdom and research
- **Materials** - Physical resources for construction
- **Mana** - Magical energy for spells and enchantments
- **Culture** - Influence and social cohesion
- **Energy** - Power for advanced technologies
- **Artifacts** - Rare pre-Sundering relics

**Faction Diplomacy:**
- Negotiate with 8 different survivor factions
- Each faction has unique ideologies and goals
- Form alliances, trade agreements, or conquest
- Navigate complex political situations

**Technology & Research:**
- Rebuild lost knowledge through 4-tier research tree
- Choose between multiple paths of development
- Story-integrated technologies with narrative weight
- Unlock new buildings, units, and capabilities

## The Campaign System: "Chronicles of Redemption"

### Campaign Structure

The game features a 7-chapter story campaign that can be played multiple times with different choices and outcomes.

#### Chapter 1: The Awakening
- **Theme:** Discovery and Basic Survival
- **Challenge:** Establish your first settlement
- **Key Mechanics:** Basic resource gathering, simple diplomacy
- **Story Focus:** Learning about the post-Sundering world
- **Victory:** Control 5 territories, reach 10,000 population

#### Chapter 2: Gathering Storms
- **Theme:** Faction Diplomacy and Early Conflicts
- **Challenge:** Navigate survivor faction politics
- **Key Mechanics:** Advanced diplomacy, military units
- **Story Focus:** First major political decisions
- **Victory:** Form 2 alliances OR defeat 2 hostile factions

#### Chapter 3: Echoes of Knowledge
- **Theme:** Technology Recovery and Ancient Mysteries
- **Challenge:** Recover dangerous pre-Sundering knowledge
- **Key Mechanics:** Advanced research, ancient site exploration
- **Story Focus:** Moral choices about forbidden knowledge
- **Victory:** Complete 2 "Lost Knowledge" research trees

#### Chapter 4: Shadows of Corruption
- **Theme:** Corruption and Moral Choices
- **Challenge:** Deal with spreading corruption
- **Key Mechanics:** Purification vs. exploitation mechanics
- **Story Focus:** Define your moral alignment
- **Victory:** Purify/harness 10 corruption sites

#### Chapter 5: Environmental Restoration
- **Theme:** Healing the Wounded World
- **Challenge:** Restore damaged ecosystems
- **Key Mechanics:** Environmental engineering, climate control
- **Story Focus:** Balance between civilization and nature
- **Victory:** Restore 3 biome types to full health

#### Chapter 6: Cultural Renaissance
- **Theme:** The Golden Age Reborn
- **Challenge:** Create a culture surpassing the ancient empire
- **Key Mechanics:** Cultural wonders, artistic movements
- **Story Focus:** Defining the values of new civilization
- **Victory:** Build 5 Great Cultural Wonders

#### Chapter 7: Dawn of a New Age
- **Theme:** Transcendence and Ultimate Destiny
- **Challenge:** Choose your civilization's ultimate fate
- **Key Mechanics:** Transcendence paths, reality manipulation
- **Story Focus:** Final choice of destiny
- **Victory:** Complete one Transcendence path

### Victory Conditions

**Three Transcendence Paths:**

1. **Path of Harmony** - Perfect balance between technology, magic, and nature
   - Sustainable civilization in eternal equilibrium
   - Preserves mortal experience while achieving perfection
   - Unlocks: Ecological paradise, immortal but grounded civilization

2. **Path of Ascension** - Transcend physical limitations entirely
   - Become beings of pure consciousness and energy
   - Leave the physical world behind for higher planes
   - Unlocks: God-like powers, dimensional travel, cosmic awareness

3. **Path of Dominion** - Achieve ultimate control over reality
   - Reshape reality according to your will
   - Rule as an absolute power over all existence
   - Unlocks: Reality manipulation, time control, universal empire

## Research & Technology System

### Research Tree Structure

**Tier 1: Survival Foundations**
- Basic Shelter, Tool Making, Organized Communities
- Simple magic and basic military units
- Foundation technologies that enable survival

**Tier 2: Advanced Development**
- Professional armies, mechanical engineering
- Advanced magic, spell structures
- Infrastructure supporting larger populations

**Tier 3: Lost Knowledge Recovery**
- Pre-Sundering technologies and magic
- Environmental restoration capabilities
- Cultural advancement and philosophy

**Tier 4: Transcendence Protocols**
- Reality-altering technologies
- Preparations for chosen victory path
- Ultimate expressions of power

### Research Branches

1. **Infrastructure** - Buildings, settlements, logistics
2. **Military** - Units, tactics, weapons
3. **Magic** - Spells, enchantments, mystical power
4. **Corruption** - Dealing with/exploiting the taint
5. **Environment** - Ecological restoration, climate control
6. **Culture** - Arts, philosophy, social advancement

### Special Research Features

- **Chapter-Gated Technologies** - Some research requires story progress
- **Prerequisite Chains** - Logical progression through tech trees
- **Multiple Resource Costs** - Different techs require different resources
- **Story Integration** - Each technology has narrative significance
- **Branching Paths** - Choices lock out other options

## Faction System

### The Eight Survivor Factions

1. **Ember Keepers**
   - *Philosophy:* Preserve the last flames of old civilization
   - *Strengths:* Knowledge preservation, defensive technologies
   - *Weaknesses:* Resistant to change, limited growth
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Stability, tradition, gradual progress

2. **Iron Covenant**
   - *Philosophy:* Strength through unity and military discipline
   - *Strengths:* Military might, organized society
   - *Weaknesses:* Authoritarian, suppresses creativity
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Order, hierarchy, conquest

3. **Verdant Circle**
   - *Philosophy:* Harmony with nature, rejection of old mistakes
   - *Strengths:* Environmental restoration, sustainable living
   - *Weaknesses:* Anti-technology, limited military
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Environmental protection, peaceful coexistence

4. **Merchant Princes**
   - *Philosophy:* Prosperity through trade and commerce
   - *Strengths:* Resource generation, economic networks
   - *Weaknesses:* Mercenary, lacks idealism
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Trade agreements, mutual profit

5. **Scholar's Enclave**
   - *Philosophy:* Knowledge above all, rational progress
   - *Strengths:* Research advancement, problem-solving
   - *Weaknesses:* Detached from practical concerns
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Intellectual exchange, research sharing

6. **Free Riders**
   - *Philosophy:* Individual freedom, reject all authority
   - *Strengths:* Adaptability, exploration, innovation
   - *Weaknesses:* Disorganized, unreliable allies
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Non-interference, personal liberty

7. **Dawn Seekers**
   - *Philosophy:* Optimistic rebuilding, bright future
   - *Strengths:* Morale, rapid growth, inspiring leadership
   - *Weaknesses:* Naive about dangers, resource intensive
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Cooperation, shared projects

8. **Shadow Guild**
   - *Philosophy:* Pragmatic survival, embrace necessary darkness
   - *Strengths:* Intelligence networks, corruption resistance
   - *Weaknesses:* Untrustworthy, morally flexible
   - *Diplomatic Preferences:* Information trade, shadowy deals

### Diplomatic Mechanics

- **Relationship Scales** - From -100 (Hostile) to +100 (Allied)
- **Faction Memory** - Past actions influence future relations
- **Ideological Compatibility** - Some factions naturally align/conflict
- **Trade Networks** - Economic relationships affect diplomacy
- **Mutual Threats** - External dangers can force cooperation

## Story & Narrative Design

### Narrative Pillars

1. **Moral Complexity** - No purely good or evil choices
2. **Consequence** - Decisions matter and have lasting impact
3. **Personal Journey** - Character growth through choices
4. **World Building** - Rich lore revealed through gameplay
5. **Hope vs. Pragmatism** - Constant tension between ideals and reality

### Story Event Types

**Major Story Events:**
- Chapter-defining moments with far-reaching consequences
- Usually 3-4 meaningful choices with different moral alignments
- Permanent effects on world state and available options

**Random Events:**
- Smaller incidents that add flavor and minor choices
- Test player's established moral framework
- Provide opportunities for resource gain/loss

**Faction Events:**
- Interactions with specific survivor groups
- Relationship-defining moments
- Can lead to alliance, war, or complex arrangements

**Discovery Events:**
- Finding ancient ruins, artifacts, or lost knowledge
- Often involve risk/reward decisions
- May unlock new research or story branches

### Choice Consequences

**Immediate Effects:**
- Resource gains/losses
- Relationship changes
- Unlocked/locked content

**Long-term Implications:**
- Available victory paths
- Faction relationships in later chapters
- Story branches and character development

**Moral Alignment Tracking:**
- Player choices build reputation across Good/Neutral/Evil spectrum
- Affects how factions react to you
- Influences available options in later events
- Determines possible endings

### Character Development

**The Last Architect (Player Character):**
- Begins as mysterious figure awakening from stasis
- Develops personality through player choices
- Can become inspiring leader, pragmatic ruler, or ruthless tyrant
- Final form depends on chosen transcendence path

**Faction Leaders:**
- Each faction has distinctive leader with personal motivations
- Relationships evolve based on player actions
- Can become trusted allies, bitter enemies, or complex frenemies
- Some may join your inner circle or become recurring antagonists

## Combat & Military System

### Commander System

Instead of controlling armies directly, players recruit and deploy **Commanders** - unique characters who lead forces and provide special abilities.

**Commander Types:**

1. **Military Commanders**
   - Specialize in direct combat and army leadership
   - Abilities: Battle tactics, troop inspiration, siege warfare
   - Examples: Veteran General, Paladin, Warlord

2. **Exploration Commanders**
   - Expert at discovering new territories and resources
   - Abilities: Pathfinding, ruin exploration, survival skills
   - Examples: Scout Master, Treasure Hunter, Archaeologist

3. **Diplomatic Commanders**
   - Skilled in negotiation and faction relations
   - Abilities: Treaty negotiation, trade deals, espionage
   - Examples: Ambassador, Spy Master, Merchant Prince

4. **Mystical Commanders**
   - Wield magical powers and arcane knowledge
   - Abilities: Spellcasting, artifact research, corruption handling
   - Examples: Arch-Mage, Corruption Cleanser, Ancient Scholar

### Combat Mechanics

**Turn-Based Tactical Combat:**
- Grid-based battlefield with terrain effects
- Unit positioning and flanking matter
- Commander abilities can turn the tide
- Environmental hazards (corruption, ancient defenses)

**Unit Types:**
- **Infantry** - Versatile ground troops, good defense
- **Cavalry** - Fast-moving, high damage, vulnerable to spears
- **Archers** - Ranged damage, weak in melee
- **Siege** - Effective against fortifications, slow
- **Magical** - Spell-based attacks, unique abilities
- **Specialists** - Engineers, medics, scouts with support roles

**Victory Conditions:**
- Eliminate enemy commander
- Control strategic points
- Survive for set number of turns
- Complete specific objectives (rescue, artifact recovery)

## Building & Settlement System

### Settlement Types

**Capitals:**
- Major population centers with all building types
- Centers of research, culture, and military production
- Require significant resources to establish and maintain
- Can support specialized districts

**Towns:**
- Medium settlements with basic amenities
- Limited building slots but self-sufficient
- Good for resource generation and population growth
- Can be upgraded to capitals with investment

**Outposts:**
- Small military or resource installations
- Minimal population, focused purpose
- Quick to establish, vulnerable to attack
- Essential for territorial control

**Sanctuaries:**
- Special settlements built on purified/restored land
- Provide unique bonuses and capabilities
- Expensive to create but very powerful
- Cannot be attacked by corruption

### Building Categories

**Infrastructure:**
- Housing, roads, utilities
- Enable population growth and happiness
- Foundation for all other activities
- Can be upgraded through research

**Economic:**
- Farms, mines, workshops, markets
- Generate resources and trade goods
- Efficiency improved by research and management
- Vulnerable to environmental damage

**Military:**
- Barracks, walls, training grounds
- Produce and upgrade military units
- Provide defensive bonuses
- Can house and resupply commanders

**Research:**
- Libraries, laboratories, academies
- Generate knowledge points for research
- Some require specific resources or artifacts
- Can specialize in different research branches

**Cultural:**
- Temples, theaters, monuments
- Generate culture points and happiness
- Attract population and improve morale
- Required for certain victory conditions

**Mystical:**
- Mage towers, ritual circles, artifact vaults
- Enable magical research and spellcasting
- Handle corruption purification/exploitation
- Require mana and specialized knowledge

### Special Buildings

**Wonders:**
- Massive projects requiring enormous resources
- Provide game-changing bonuses
- Often required for victory conditions
- Can only build one of each type

**Ancient Installations:**
- Discovered and restored pre-Sundering buildings
- Unique capabilities not reproducible
- Often require dangerous expeditions to claim
- May have hidden costs or requirements

## Progression & Replayability

### Campaign Progression

**Linear Story Backbone:**
- Core narrative progresses through fixed chapters
- Ensures dramatic pacing and story coherence
- Major events occur at predetermined points

**Branching Choices:**
- Player decisions create different story branches
- Multiple solutions to each chapter's challenges
- Affects available options in later chapters

**Research Variability:**
- Different research paths lead to different capabilities
- Can't research everything in single playthrough
- Encourages different strategic approaches

### Replayability Features

**Multiple Victory Paths:**
- Three transcendence endings with different requirements
- Each path requires different research and story choices
- Significantly different gameplay experiences

**Moral Alignment Variations:**
- Good/Neutral/Evil choices lead to different stories
- Faction relationships change based on alignment
- Some content only available to specific alignments

**Faction Focus:**
- Can prioritize different factions across playthroughs
- Each faction offers unique benefits and story content
- Some faction combinations create special scenarios

**Research Specialization:**
- Different research priorities create different civilizations
- Magic-focused vs. technology-focused vs. balanced approaches
- Each specialization unlocks unique capabilities

**Challenge Modes:**
- Optional difficulty modifiers for experienced players
- Limited resources, stronger enemies, time pressure
- Special achievements for completing challenges

### New Game Plus Features

**Legacy Bonuses:**
- Previous accomplishments provide small starting bonuses
- Encourages multiple playthroughs
- Never game-breaking, just quality of life improvements

**Unlocked Content:**
- Some research requires knowledge from previous campaigns
- Hidden story events revealed after certain achievements
- Advanced start options for experienced players

**Achievement System:**
- Complex objectives spanning multiple playthroughs
- Rewards for specific story choices or strategies
- Collectible lore and artwork unlocks

## Conclusion

**Ashes of Aeloria** represents an ambitious fusion of deep strategy gameplay with meaningful narrative choice. By focusing on the rebuilding of civilization rather than its destruction, the game offers a unique perspective in the post-apocalyptic genre while delivering the complex decision-making that strategy fans crave.

The game's success will depend on creating authentic emotional investment in both the strategic challenges and the story consequences, ensuring that players feel their choices matter not just mechanically but narratively. The multi-layered approach to replayability, combined with a strong foundation for community content creation, positions the game for long-term engagement and potential franchise development.

The technical and artistic execution must match the ambition of the design, creating a world that feels both devastated and beautiful, ancient and forward-looking, challenging and ultimately hopeful. When players complete their chosen transcendence path, they should feel they have not just won a game, but participated in an epic story of redemption and renewal.
